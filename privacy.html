Pichurri — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 31 May 2026

Pichurri ("the app", "we", "us") is a gut-health tracking app that helps you log food and symptoms and visualise patterns. This policy explains what we collect, why, where it is stored, who processes it on our behalf, and the choices you have.

The app is operated by Pilar Esteban, based in New South Wales, Australia. For any privacy question or request, contact us at pilar.esteban@gmail.com.

1. What we collect

  • Account details — your email address (used to sign in and to identify your account).
  • Food entries — the meals you log: text descriptions, the ingredient list, and, if you use the AI features, the meal photos or voice descriptions you submit.
  • Symptom logs — the symptoms you record (type, intensity, timing, Bristol stool scale and similar notes you choose to enter).
  • Companion / sharing data — if you link a partner account, your shared Pichurri and diary entries are visible to that linked user.
  • Subscription status — whether you have an active Pichurri MAX subscription (we do not receive or store your card details — Apple handles payment).
  • Usage and device data — basic analytics events (e.g. screens viewed, features used), app version and device type, to understand and improve the app.

2. How we use your data

  • To provide the core tracking, diary, insights and game features.
  • To run AI food analysis when you use a paid (MAX) AI feature.
  • To manage your subscription and send app notifications you've enabled.
  • To diagnose problems and improve the product.

We do not sell your personal data, and we do not use your data for advertising.

3. Where your data is stored

Your account, food, symptom and diary data are stored in our database hosted on Supabase, in a European Union (EU) region. Data is encrypted in transit. We keep automated backups for up to 30 days for recovery purposes.

4. Sub-processors

We use the following third parties to operate the app. Each receives only the data needed for its function.

ProviderPurposeData shared
Supabase Database & storage hosting (EU) Account email, food entries, symptoms, diary, subscription status
Anthropic (Claude) AI food analysis — paid (MAX) features only Only the meal text, photo or voice description you submit for analysis. No name or email is sent.
PostHog Product analytics Pseudonymous usage events, app version, device type
RevenueCat & Apple Subscription management & payment Purchase/receipt and a pseudonymous user identifier. Card details are handled by Apple, never by us.
About the AI features. When you use AI food analysis, only the meal content you submit (text, photo or voice) is sent to Anthropic to identify ingredients. It is not linked to your name or email, and it is not used to train Anthropic's models. AI features are part of the paid Pichurri MAX tier; on the free tier no data is sent to Anthropic.

5. Your choices and rights

  • Delete your account and data — at any time from Profile → Delete Account in the app. This removes your data from our active database; backup copies roll off within 30 days.
  • Export your data — from Profile → Export my data you can download a copy of your data.
  • Access and correction — you can view and edit your entries in the app, or contact us for help.
  • Notifications — turn reminders on or off in the app or in iOS Settings.

Australian users have rights under the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles. Because data is stored in the EU, EU/EEA users also have rights under the GDPR (access, rectification, erasure, portability and objection). To exercise any of these, email pilar.esteban@gmail.com.

6. Children

Pichurri is not directed at children under 16, and we do not knowingly collect data from them.

7. Medical disclaimer

Pichurri is a self-tracking tool, not a medical device. It does not provide medical advice or diagnosis. Always consult a qualified health professional about your symptoms.

8. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. Material changes will be reflected by the "Last updated" date above.
